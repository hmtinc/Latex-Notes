\section{Likelihood Intervals}
Instead of finding the "most likely" value of \(\theta\) (MLE), we are trying to find the plausible values of \(\theta\) 

\begin{definition}
Let \(p \in (0,1)\), a \(100 \cdot p \%\) likelihood interval for \(\theta = \{\theta : R(\theta) \geq p \} \) where \(R(\theta)\) = Relative likelihood function
\end{definition}

\subsection{Conventions}
\begin{itemize}
\item \(R(\theta) \geq 0.5 \implies \) Very Plausible  
\item \(0.1 < R(\theta) < 0.5 \implies\)  Plausible
\item \(0.01 \leq R(\theta) < 0.1 \implies\) Implausible
\item \(R(\theta) \leq 0.01 \implies\) Very Implausible
\end{itemize}
