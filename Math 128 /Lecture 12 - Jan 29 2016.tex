\section{Arc Length Example}

\begin{ex}
Determine the length of the of \(x = \frac{2}{3} (y^2 + 1) ^\frac{3}{2}\) from y = -2 to y = 1
\[ \begin{aligned} L = \int_{-2}^{1} \sqrt{ 1+ (2y(y^2+1)^\frac{1}{2})^2} dy  & = \int_{-2}^1 \sqrt{1 + 4y^2(y^2 + 1)} dy \\
& = \int_{-2}^1 \sqrt{1 + 4y^4 + 4y^2} dy \\
& =  \int_{-2}^1 \sqrt{(2y^2 + 1)^2} dy  \\ 
& = \int_{-2}^1 (2y^2 + 1) dy \\
& = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{2}{3}y^3 + y\end{bmatrix}_{-2}^{1} \\ & = 9 \end{aligned}
\]
\end{ex}

\section{Area of Surface of Revolution}

Formula : \\
about x - axis : SA = \(\int 2\pi y ds\) where \( ds = \sqrt{1 + (\frac{dy}{dx})^2} dx\) if \( y = f(x) , a \leq y \leq b\) \\ 
about y - axis : SA = \(\int 2\pi x ds\) where \( ds = \sqrt{1 + (\frac{dx}{dy})^2} dy\) if \( x = g(y) , c \leq y \leq d\) \\
