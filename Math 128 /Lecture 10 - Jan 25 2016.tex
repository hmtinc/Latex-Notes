\section{Volumes of Washers and Discs} 
\begin{ex}
Find the volume obtained by rotating the region bounded by \( y = x^2 + 4 , y = 2x^2\) about y = 0  \\

The two curves intersect at \( x = \pm 2 \), so rotating the rectangles of width dx about y = 0 will result in a washer like object with a certain volume\\ 
by taking the sum of all the washers from x = -2 to x = 2 , we get 
$$ V = \int_{-2}^2  ( \pi (x^2 + 4)^2 - \pi (2x)^2) dx $$
$$ = \frac{1024\pi}{15} $$
\end{ex}

\textbf{In general : } When using disks/washers \\
$$ V = \int_{a}^b A(x) or V = \int_{a}^b A(y) $$ 
where A(x)/A(y) is the cross sectional area of the solid. 

\section{Volumes of Cylindrical Shells}

\textbf{In general : }  the volume of a solid obtained by rotating the region under y = f(x) about y axis for x = a to x = b 
$$ V = \int_{a}^b  2\pi f(x) dx $$
