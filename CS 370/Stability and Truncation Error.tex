\lecture{6}{Stability and Truncation Error }{Christopher Batty}{Harsh Mistry}

\section{Stability of Time-Stepping Schemes}
\begin{enumerate}
\item Apply a given time stepping scheme to out test equation.
\item Find the closed form of its numerical solution. 
\item Find the conditions on the timestep \(h\) that ensures stability (error approaching zero) 
\end{enumerate}

\section{Determining Local Truncation Error}
Recall that local truncation error is 
$$ LTE = y(t_{n+1}) - y_{n+1}$$
where \(y(t_{n+1})\) is exact solution and \(y_{n+1}\) is approximate solution. \\
Assuming exact right-hand-side data, this is the error from taking one step.

\subsection{Process}
Given a time-stepping scheme, \(y_{n+1} = RHS\)
\begin{enumerate}
\item Replace approximations on RHS with exact versions
\item Taylor expand all RHS quantities about time \(t_n\) (if necessary)  
\item Taylor expand the exact solution \(y(t_{n+1}\) to compare against.
\item Compute difference \(y(y_{n+1}) - y_{n+1}\). Lowest degree non-cancelling power of \(h\) gives the local truncation error 
\end{enumerate}
